METALLICA

Metallica is an American heavy metal band formed in Los Angeles, California, in 1981. The band was founded by drummer Lars Ulrich and guitarist/vocalist James Hetfield. Metallica is known for their aggressive musical style, characterized by fast tempos, powerful guitar riffs, and socially conscious lyrics. Over the years, Metallica has become one of the most successful and influential heavy metal bands in the world. They have released numerous albums, many of which have achieved multi-platinum status. Some of their most famous albums include "Master of Puppets," "Ride the Lightning," "Metallica" (also known as the Black Album), and "…And Justice for All."

SLIPKNOT

Originally from Des Moines, Iowa, Slipknot are an American heavy metal band. The band's aggressive and chaotic sound has made them famous, and so has their spectacular live show. The members of Slipknot are instantly recognizable by their signature jumpsuits and masks.

Heavy Metal Music

Heavy metal (or simply metal) is a genre of rock music that developed in the late 1960s and early 1970s, largely in the United Kingdom and United States. With roots in blues rock, psychedelic rock and acid rock, heavy metal bands developed a thick, monumental sound characterized by distorted guitars, extended guitar solos, emphatic beats and loudness.
